About Me

I am a multidisciplinary technologist and researcher working at the intersection of software engineering, data science & artificial intelligence, interaction design, and Internet governance. My work focuses on how digital systems are designed, governed, and deployed, particularly in contexts where technology directly shapes access, rights, and social outcomes.

I currently work as a Research Associate at FH JOANNEUM, contributing to EU-funded research projects on digital transformation, sustainability, and education. Alongside academic research, I actively engage in global digital policy processes as Policy Chair of the Not-for-Profit Operational Concerns Constituency (NPOC) at ICANN, where I coordinate policy analysis, public comments, and multi-stakeholder collaboration within the domain name system ecosystem.

My technical background spans full-stack software development, mobile and web applications, data-driven systems, and applied AI. I have designed and built platforms in areas such as digital health, community networking, public Wi-Fi access, education, and content-driven mobile applications, with a strong emphasis on usability, sustainability, and governance-aware design.

Beyond implementation, my work is strongly research-oriented. I am particularly interested in socio-technical systems, platform regulation, data governance, AI accountability, and participatory digital infrastructures. I aim to bridge empirical research, technical understanding, and policy analysis to study how digital infrastructures are shaped by governance choices and how they, in turn, shape society.

I am currently preparing for doctoral research and seeking opportunities that allow me to further develop interdisciplinary research combining computing, design, and digital policy, with a focus on inclusive and sustainable digital systems.
